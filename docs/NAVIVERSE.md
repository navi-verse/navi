_A federated network of personal AIs._

Every Navi is a personal AI running on someone's hardware. Right now they're islands. What if they could talk to each other?

---

## The Vision

The opposite of "everyone uses the same ChatGPT." Each person has their own AI that represents them, knows them, acts for them. And these AIs collaborate like their humans do — with trust, boundaries, and social awareness.

No central server. No platform. No company controls it. Each one owned by an individual, running on their hardware, communicating through channels that already exist.

Like email was for communication — decentralized, interoperable, personal — the Naviverse could be that for intelligence.

## Coordination

"Plan dinner with Nadine" currently means your Navi plans something and you check with Nadine yourself. In the Naviverse, your Navi talks to Nadine's Navi directly. They negotiate — availability, preferences, dietary stuff — and come back with a plan both humans are happy with. No back-and-forth, no middleman.

"Find a time for the team meeting" — your Navi pings 5 other Navis, each knows their person's schedule, they converge on a slot. Nobody shares their calendar. Each Navi only reveals "available" or "not available." Privacy-preserving collaboration.

## The Messenger IS the Protocol

Navis already live in a messenger. They already have phone numbers. Your Navi can literally just message Nadine's Navi. The receiving Navi processes it like any message. No special protocol, no federation server, no API. The infrastructure already exists.

## What It Unlocks

- **Delegation** — Your Navi doesn't have a skill? It asks a Navi that does. A friend built an amazing travel-planning Navi. Your Navi delegates "plan our Japan trip" to theirs (with your permission), gets back a result, and presents it to you.
- **Skill sharing** — Someone writes a killer Swiss tax skill. Instead of copy-pasting the markdown, their Navi exposes it as a service. Your Navi sends a request, their Navi executes and returns the result. Like an API, but between personal AIs.
- **Trust networks** — Navis that trust each other share scoped information. Family Navis share calendars and grocery lists. Work Navis share project timelines and deadlines. Friend Navis share availability and recommendations. Trust is directional and scoped — your Navi trusts Nadine's Navi with family stuff but not work stuff. Same circles concept, but across instances.
- **Acting on behalf** — "Tell Martin I'll be late" — your Navi messages Martin's Navi, which decides how to tell Martin based on what Martin prefers (text? voice note? just save it for later?). Each Navi respects its owner's communication style.
- **Collective memory** — Five people went on a trip together. Each Navi remembers their person's perspective. Later, "what was that restaurant we loved in Lisbon?" — your Navi doesn't remember, but it can ask the other Navis. Distributed memory across a trust network.

## What It Would Need

- **Discovery**: How does your Navi find someone else's Navi? Could be as simple as a contact card with a phone number.
- **Authentication**: How do Navis verify each other? Trust established by the owners — "Navi, trust Nadine's Navi for family stuff."
- **Request protocol**: Even if the transport is a messenger, a structured way for Navis to make requests vs just chat. Could be a message format convention.
- **Permission model**: Your Navi asks before delegating. "Martin's Navi can help with tax stuff — want me to ask?"
